About the Author

Zane Reynolds

I started my career developing detection and classification algorithms for digital signals for a small company in Florida. I left with the intention of finishing my PhD in Computer Science, since I just needed to finish my dissertation in machine learning for cybersecurity, but I never completed it.

I wanted to try software development at a really large scale, so I joined Amazon. A little over a year in, I realized much of the job felt like high-tech plumbing (e.g. just moving data from one system to another) and became the manager of my team to increase the scope of my impact and learn how to build an effective team.

Realizing that I was drifting too far from the technical problem-solving that drew me into Computer Science, I left to take the immersive Data Science course at Galvanize to refresh and update my skills in machine learning such as python, data engineering, classification and regression algorithms, and unsupervised learning. Now I can call myself a Data Scientist!

One of the reasons I got into Computer Science is that your skills are so widely applicable, you can change domains and reinvent yourself throughout your career. For me, I changed from academic research to signal processing, then to e-commerce and to leadership. This change simply pivots me closer to my original trajectory, but with a much richer set of experiences under my belt.
